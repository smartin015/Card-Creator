Welcome!

Using the Handbook

Here's a couple tricks to help you learn faster:

  • Keep this guide open on your phone as a quick reference.
  • Use the menu on the top left to quickly jump to a section.

The Cards

Tap a card to learn more

Explorers

Explorers represent you and your party. Every explorer has their own role and special skills - it's up to you to realize them.

  1. Name: what your character is called in the realm. You'll have to fill in this dashed section yourself.
  2. Health: the amount of health you start with. It's the number next to the heart on the top right.
  3. Skills: usable at any time.

Encounters

Encounters are creatures, beings, or environmental hazards. Your Guide may play these cards to represent key story characters, or enemies lying in wait.

  1. Health: the enemy's starting health.
  2. Strengths/Weaknesses: against types of damage or situations.
  3. Threat: a roman numeral indicating difficulty.
  4. Surge: their special attack or action.

Abilities

Abilities are actions you make during combat. They can be offensive or defensive, target yourself or others, and may or may not fail catastrophically when used.

  1. Damage or Defense: how much you hurt others or protect yourself.
  2. Damage Type: the type of damage dealt. Some enemies may be resistant to certain types.
  3. Risk: how likely you'll succeed.
  4. Target(s): how many enemies or allies are affected.

Loot

Loot are tools, weapons, and other tangible items. They can be used at any time to heal, damage enemies, or advance the plot.

  1. Usage: when or how often it can be used.
  2. Effects: choose from one of these when you use the Loot.
  3. Cost: the trade value in gold coins.

Titles

Titles are given for exceptional roleplaying. If you impress your Guide with your roleplaying abilities, they may grant you a title of your very own.

  1. Name: this is written by your Guide.
  2. Passive: a small bonus you receive all the time.
  3. Release: a powerful one-off ability that destroys the Title.

Setting Up

1. Choose an Explorer

Everyone in your party needs an Explorer Card.

  • Select one Explorer from the deck and pass the deck along.
  • Name your character by writing the name at the top.
  • Keep this card face up in front of you.

2. Learn 6 Abilities

Pick your abilities from a single Ability deck such as Ranged or Magic.

  • Draw three Ability cards.
  • Keep one that you think would be the most useful.
  • Discard the others at the bottom of the deck.
  • Repeat this until you have six Ability cards in your hand.
  • Place them face down in a deck near your Explorer card.

3. Gear Up

Your Guide will lay out Loot for you to buy. You have 25 gold to spend.

  • Buy a weapon to match your Abilities (e.g. a wand for Magic)
  • Buy healing items to stay healthy during and after battle.
  • Buy other items that interest you. They'll probably come in handy.
  • Save some gold! Write it down, so you can spend it later.

Exploring

Use your Guide

Your Guide controls the world, and you affect the world through them.

  • The Guide sets up the story, environment, and challenges for you to overcome.
  • Ask them questions about your quest and your surroundings if you're unclear on what to do next.
  • You may control your character's actions, but your Guide controls the world's reaction. Exercise caution.

Get Creative

Expedition is a game with open rules:

  • You can make your character do whatever you want. Just tell your Guide, and it'll happen.
  • Use the environment to your advantage - if you find a cloth tent, try setting it on fire or ripping through it.
  • Creativity applies to combat, too! Your action doesn't have to be an Ability card every round.
  • The Skills section of your Explorer card can help you brainstorm.

Stay Prepared

Your next enemy encounter is closer than you think...

  • Keep yourself healed with Loot or bed rest.
  • Plan with your party on how to handle the next Encounter.
  • Sort your Ability deck, and think of your best opening moves.

Combat

Overview

Every combat is a series of Rounds, and continues until one side prevails.

  • Every round, you'll play an Ability or make up your own action.
  • Choose your abilities quickly - damage increases the longer you deliberate.
  • There are no take-backsies. Once you've played a card, that decision is final.
  • Don't forget: creative roleplaying may earn you a Title!

Rounds

Draw three abilities from the top of your ability deck.

Play an Ability from your hand. Some cards may let you play more than one. You may also use your weapon's basic attack or improvise an action.

Discard the remaining cards into a separate pile from your Ability deck.

Roll a D20 and resolve your ability.

Shuffle your discarded cards back into your deck whenever your deck runs low.

Round Resolution

Your roll must meet or exceed the card's risk for your ability to succeed.

Rolling a 20 is a critical success. Your ability's effects are doubled.

Rolling a 1 is a critical failure. Your ability's effects are reversed. The negative effects target you; positive effects target the enemy.

Be sure to roleplay what your ability did, or else it didn't happen!

Offensive Abilities

Offensive Abilities attack and damage an enemy.

  • If you succeed, choose an enemy or enemies to take the damage.
  • Take chare when choosing, as some enemies may be resistant or immune to Ability's damage type.
  • Some enemies are weak to certain damage types. The Guide may grant extra damage if the ability qualifies.

Defensive Abilities

Defensive Abilities take damage in your stead.

  • If you succeed, place it in front of your character.
  • On this and subsequent rounds, you may choose to transfer your damage to the ability.
  • Some Abilities defend your entire party - these can change the tide of battle, but are often risky to cast.

Ancillary Abilities

Ancillary Abilities are neither offensive or defensive.

  • Some cards affect your hand, allowing you to draw or play more cards in a single round.
  • Others affect your risk or rolling ability, and can make hard abilities easier next round.
  • Still others can stun enemies, and add or remove strengths or weaknesses.

Teamwork

Your party can accomplish great and heroic feats, but you must work together to survive.

  • Plan to have at least one defensive party member, so you can outlast enemies in longer battles.
  • You may defend an ally by taking their damage for the round, keeping them healthy and their abilities uninterrupted.
  • When you find Loot, consider who needs it the most before taking it.
  • Some quests may pit your party against each other. In this case, teamwork with your remaining allies is even more critical.

Damage

At the end of every round, you may take damage. This damage adds up.

  • If your character runs out of Health, they are knocked out for several rounds.
  • KO'd characters may rejoin combat after a few rounds, at the Guide's discretion.
  • If the entire party gets wiped out, the enemy (i.e. the Guide) determines your fate.
  • KO'd characters usually awaken with 1 Health if their party survives the encounter.
