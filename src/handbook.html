Welcome!

Using the Handbook

When playing, you'll make snap decisions that affect you and those around you. Know what you're doing before you dive in.

  • Keep this guide open on your phone as a quick reference.
  • Use the nav menu on the top left to quickly jump to a section.

The Cards

Explorers

Explorers are what you and your party are.

  • Name, what you're called in the realm. You'll have to fill in this dashed section yourself.
  • Health, the amount of health you start with. It's the number next to the heart on the top right.
  • Skills, usable at any time.
  • Gold, for buying items or favors.

Encounters

Encounters are foes or traps lying in wait.

  • Health, the amount of damage needed to defeat them.
  • Threat, a roman numeral that indicates their difficulty.
  • Surge, their special attack or action.
  • Strengths/Weaknesses, to types of damage or situations.

Abilities

Abilities are things you do during battle.

  • Risk, how likely you'll fail.
  • Damage or Defense, how much you hurt others or protect yourself.
  • Damage Type is how the enemy is effected.
  • Target(s) indicates how many targets are affected.

Loot

Loot are tools, weapons, and other tangible things.

  • Usage, when or how often it can be used.
  • Effects, which you choose from when it's used.
  • Cost, the trade value in gold coins.

Titles

Titles are rewards for exceptional role playing.

  • Passive, a small bonus you receive all the time.
  • Release, a powerful one-off ability that destroys the Title.

Setting Up

1. Choose an Explorer

Everyone in your party needs an Explorer Card.

  • Select one Explorer from the deck and pass the deck along.
  • Pick a name for your character and write it at the top.
  • Keep this card face up in front of you.

2. Learn 6 Abilities

Pick your abilities from a single Ability deck.

  • Draw three Ability cards.
  • Keep one that you think would be useful.
  • Discard the others at the bottom of the deck.
  • Repeat this until you have six Ability cards in your hand.
  • Place them face down in a deck near your Explorer card.

3. Gear Up

Your Guide will lay out Loot for you. You have 25 gold to spend.

  • Buy a weapon to match your Abilities (e.g. a wand for Magic)
  • Buy healing items to stay healthy during and after battle.
  • Buy a few other interesting items for fun.
  • Save some gold! Write it down, so you can spend it later.

Exploring

Use your Guide

Your Guide controls the world. They set up the story, environment, and challenges for you to overcome.

Get Creative

You don't have to just play your given Skills or Abilities.

  • Use the situation to your advantage - if you find a cloth tent, try setting it on fire or ripping through it.

Stay Prepared

Your next enemy encounter is closer than you think...

  • Keep yourself healed with Loot, Abilities, or bed rest.
  • Plan with your Party on how to handle the next Encounter.
  • Sort your Ability deck, with your first moves on top.

Combat

Fighting

Every combat is a series of Rounds, and continues until one side prevails.

  • Every round, you'll play an Ability or make up your own action.
  • Choose your abilities quickly - damage increases the longer you deliberate.
  • There are no take-backsies. Once you've played a card, that decision is final.

Attack

  • Abilities with damage are directed at enemies to cause them harm.

Defense

  • Abilities with defense (such as wards and certain musics) are played in front of your character and take damage instead of your character.

A Single Round

  • Draw three abilities from your ability deck
  • Play an Ability from your hand. Some cards may allow you to play more than one.
  • Discard your hand of remaining abilities into a separate pile from your Ability deck.
  • Roll a D20 and resolve your ability.

Resolving the Round

  • Your roll must meet or exceed the risk for the ability for your ability to succeed.
  • Its effects are doubled if a 20 is rolled
  • If you roll a 1 it critically fails (the negative effects target you; positive effects target the enemy).
  • Be sure to roleplay what your ability did, or else it didn't happen.

Dying

At the end of every round, you may take damage. This damage adds up.

  • If your character runs out of Health, they are "knocked out" for several rounds.
  • KO'd characters may rejoin combat after a few turns, at the Guide's discretion.
  • If the entire party gets wiped out, the enemy (i.e. the Guide) determines your fate.
  • KO'd characters usually awaken with 1 Health if their party survives the encounter.
