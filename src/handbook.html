Welcome!

This handbook only takes a couple minutes to read through, and will teach you everything you need to know in order to enjoy the game as an Adventurer.

Want to be the Guide, or to learn more? See our Guide for Guides.

The Cards

Adventurer Cards

Adventurers represent you and your party. Every adventurer has their own role and special skills - it's up to you to realize them.

  1. Name: what your character is called in the realm. You'll have to fill in this dashed section yourself.
  2. Health: the amount of health you start with is next to the heart. Use a paper clip to keep track of your current health by sliding it along the edge.
  3. Level: you start at level 1. If you earn a level through your adventuring, you'll flip your card over to level 2.
  4. Bonuses: you get a bonus to any roleplaying rolls that you can make fit these categories.

Encounter Cards

Encounters are the various beings played by your Guide to represent key story characters, or enemies lying in wait.

  1. Health: the enemy's starting health.
  2. Strengths/Weaknesses: against types of damage or situations.
  3. Surge: their special attack or action.
  4. Tier: a roman numeral indicating difficulty.

Ability Cards

Abilities are actions you make during combat. They can be offensive or defensive, target yourself or others, and may or may not fail catastrophically when used.

  1. Damage Type: the type of damage dealt. Enemies may be weak to certain types.
  2. Risk: You need to roll at least as high as this number to succeed.
  3. Target(s): how many enemies or allies are affected.
  4. Damage or Health: how much you hurt or heal your target.

Loot Cards

Loot are tools, weapons, and other tangible items. They can be used at any time to heal, damage enemies, or advance the plot.

  1. Cost: the buy / sell value.
  2. Usage: how often it can be used.
  3. Effects: choose from one of these when you use the Loot.

Setting Up

1. Choose an Explorer

Everyone in your party needs an Explorer Card.

  • Select one Explorer from the deck and pass the deck along.
  • Keep this card face up in front of you.

2. Learn 6 Abilities

Pick your abilities from a single Ability deck such as Ranged or Magic.

  • Draw three Ability cards.
  • Keep one that you think would be the most useful.
  • Discard the others to the bottom of the deck.
  • Repeat until you have six Ability cards in your hand.
  • Place them face down in a deck near your Explorer card.

Exploring

Your Environment

Your Guide controls the world, and you affect the world through them.

  • The Guide sets up the story, environment, and challenges for you to overcome.
  • Ask them questions about your quest and your surroundings if you're unclear on what to do next.
  • You may control your character's actions, but your Guide controls the world's reaction. Exercise caution.

Get Creative

Expedition is a game with open rules:

  • You can make your character do whatever you want. Just tell your Guide, and it'll happen.
  • Use the environment to your advantage - if you find a cloth tent, try setting it on fire or ripping through it.
  • Creativity applies to combat, too! Your action doesn't have to be an Ability card every round.
  • Try crafting your roleplaying action to fit one of the Skills on your Explorer card to captialize on your roll bonuses.

Combat

Overview

Every Encounter is a series of Rounds. Combat continues until one side prevails.

  • Every round, you'll play an Ability or make up your own action.
  • Choose your abilities quickly - damage increases the longer you deliberate.
  • There are no take-backsies. Once you've played a card, that decision is final.

Rounds

Draw three abilities from the top of your ability deck.

Play an Ability from your hand. Some cards may let you play more than one.

Roll a D20 and resolve your ability.

Shuffle all of your Ability cards back into your deck.

Round Resolution

Your roll must meet or exceed the card's risk for your ability to succeed.

Rolling a 20 is a critical success. Unless otherwise indicated, your ability's effects are doubled.

Rolling a 1 is a critical failure. Unless otherwise indicated, your ability's effects are reversed. The negative effects target you; positive effects target the enemy.

Be sure to roleplay what your adventurer did!

Damage

At the end of every round, you may take damage. This damage adds up.

  • If your character runs out of Health, they are knocked out until the end of the Encounter or a fellow adventurer revives them.
  • KO'd characters may rejoin combat after a few rounds at the Guide's discretion.
  • If the entire party gets wiped out, the enemy (i.e. the Guide) determines your fate.
  • KO'd characters usually awaken at half Health if their party survives the encounter.
