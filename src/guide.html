The Cards

Explorers

Explorers are yourself and your party.
  • Name, what you're called in the realm.
  • Health, the amount of health you start with.
  • Skills, usable at any time.
  • Gold, for buying items or favors.

Encounters

are foes or traps lying in wait.

  • Health, the amount of damage needed to defeat them.
  • Threat, a roman numeral that indicates their difficulty.
  • Surge, their special attack or action.
  • Strengths/Weaknesses, to types of damage or situations.

Abilities

are things you do during battle.

  • Risk, how likely you'll fail.
  • Damage or Defense, how much you hurt others or protect yourself.
  • Damage Type is how the enemy is effected.
  • Target(s) indicates how many targets are affected.

Loot

are tools, weapons, and other tangible things.

  • Usage, when or how often it can be used.
  • Effects, which you choose from when it's used.
  • Cost, the trade value in gold coins.

Titles

are rewards for exceptional role playing.

  • Passive, a small bonus you receive all the time.
  • Release, a powerful one-off ability that destroys the Title.

Setup

1. Choose an Explorer

Everyone in your party needs an Explorer Card.

  • Select one Explorer from the deck and pass the deck along.
  • Pick a Name and write it at the top.
  • Keep this card face up on the table.

2. Learn 6 Abilities

Pick your starting lineup from one Ability deck, choosing a few with Risk 10 or less.

  • Draw three cards.
  • Pick one Ability card and keep it.
  • Discard the others at the bottom of the deck.
  • Repeat until you have six cards.
  • Place them face down near your Explorer card.

3. Gear Up

Your Guide will lay out Loot for you. You have 25 gold to spend.

  • Buy a weapon to match your Abilities (e.g. a wand for Magic)
  • Buy healing items to stay healthy during and after battle.
  • Buy a few other items for fun.
  • Save some gold! You can write it down and spend it later.

Explore

Guide

Your Guide controls the world. They set up the story, environment, and challenges for you to overcome.

Prepare

for combat.

  • Keep yourself healed with Loot, Abilities, or bed rest.
  • Plan with your Party on how to handle the next Encounter.
  • Sort your Ability deck, with your first moves on top.

Combat

Combat is broken into Rounds. Every round, you'll play an Ability or make up your own action.

Your roll must meet or exceed the risk for the ability to succeed. Every Ability has two numbers on the top right: damage or defense on the top, and risk on the bottom. Abilities with defense (such as wards and certain musics) are played in front of your character and take damage instead of your character. Abilities with damage are directed at enemies to cause them harm.

When fighting it's important to choose your abilities quickly, and there are no take-backsies and your damage taken increases the longer you deliberate.

  • Draw three abilities from your ability deck
  • Play an Ability from your hand. Some cards may allow you to play more than one.
  • Discard your hand of remaining abilities into a separate pile from your Ability deck.
  • Roll a D20 and resolve your ability.

If the result is at least the Risk of the card, you can use the action. Its effects are doubled if a 20 is rolled; if you roll a 1 it critically fails (the negative effects target you; positive effects target the enemy). Be sure to roleplay what your ability did, or else it didn't happen.

Recover

Loot can be used at any time.

Not Dying

Avoid Damage

After every combat round, you will likely take damage - and this damage adds up. If your character runs out of Health, they are considered knocked out for several rounds. They may rejoin the combat at the Guide's discretion. If your entire party gets wiped out, the enemy (i.e. the Guide) can do pretty much anything they want with you. KO'd characters usually awaken with 1 Health. If you are knocked out but your party survives the fight, they'll wait for you to regain consciousness and you'll awaken significantly weaker - with only a 1 on the physical health scale.

It greatly helps to use abilities with Defense to take damage in your stead. If you don't have a defensive ability, ask other party members to take your share of the damage for that round.

Get Creative

Similarly, you aren't limited to what actions you have memorized. Use the situation to your advantage! Your Guide can advise you on the relative risks and merits of improvised actions. Deviating from what you've practiced does carry a certain amount of extra risk, though.

Arming yourself with training, equipment, and combat knowledge is the best way to stay alive as you travel the realm. We've provided you with the basics, but it's up to you to experience the world and grow stronger within it. Good luck, and try not to die!
